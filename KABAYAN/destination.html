Mount Tabayoc

Mount Tabayoc is Luzon’s second highest peak, surrounded by pristine mossy forests and offering panoramic views of the Cordillera mountains.

Mount Pulag

Mount Pulag is the highest peak in Luzon, famous for its “sea of clouds” and diverse ecosystems. A UNESCO heritage candidate and trekking favorite.

Kabayan Fire Mummies

The Kabayan Fire Mummies are preserved through a unique smoking process. These burial caves are a National Cultural Treasure.

Four Lakes of Kabayan
Lake Bulalacao

A serene mountain lake surrounded by lush forests, perfect for eco-tourism and birdwatching.

Lake Latepngap

Known for its tranquil waters and scenic mountain backdrop, ideal for quiet reflection.

Lake Inmulung

A hidden gem nestled in Kabayan’s highlands, offering pristine natural beauty.

Lake Tabeyo

The largest of Kabayan’s lakes, often used for local fishing and community gatherings.
