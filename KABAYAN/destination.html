Mount Tabayoc

About: Luzon’s second highest peak, surrounded by mossy forests and offering panoramic Cordillera views. A quieter alternative to Pulag for trekkers.

Coordinator: Kabayan Tourism Office

Homestay Options: Tabayoc Eco Homestay | Cordillera Lodge

Mount Pulag

About: The highest peak in Luzon, famous for its “sea of clouds” and diverse ecosystems. A UNESCO heritage candidate and trekking favorite.

Coordinator: Mount Pulag Park Management

Homestay Options: Pulag Base Homestay | Kabayan Guesthouse

Timbak Caves

About: Sacred burial caves in Kabayan, known for their cultural significance and ancient traditions. Part of the UNESCO tentative list.

Coordinator: Kabayan Tourism Coordinator

Homestay Options: Timbak Village Homestay | Cordillera Guesthouse

Four Lakes of Kabayan

About: Kabayan’s four lakes — Bulalacao, Latepngap, Inmulung, and Tabeyo — each offer unique natural beauty, from birdwatching to fishing and community gatherings.

Coordinator: Kabayan Eco-Tourism Office

Homestay Options: Lake Tabeyo Homestay | Bulalacao Eco-Lodge

Tinongchol Burial Cave

About: Coffins carved from pinewood, reflecting ancient burial traditions of Kabayan. A sacred heritage site.

Coordinator: Kabayan Heritage Office

Homestay Options: Tinongchol Homestay | Kabayan Cultural Lodge

Opdas Burial Cave

About: A cave housing centuries-old skeletal remains, offering a glimpse into Kabayan’s past and its burial customs.

Coordinator: Kabayan Cultural Coordinator

Homestay Options: Opdas Village Homestay | Cordillera Heritage Lodge
